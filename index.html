What's the deal with Leipzig?

Leipzig; the city of politics and poo.

The beautiful garden of our hostel on a sunny day, perfect for a quick nap.

We stayed at a beautiful carefully decorated hostel, with a big green garden and a local pet dog called Taxi. Our home was a Utopia, a safe space, a haven. And outside our heavenly hostel, lurked the cold hearted, mysterious passages of Leipzig city. After a few hours of pondering, I overcame my fear of the unknown and decided to leave my beautiful bubble and before I knew it, I had become part of many who roam the streets in silence. And it was fun, it was fine and it became a new habit of mine; to go solo, leave at night or during the day and explore my new home. But there was one obstacle:

Doodoo on every damn corner.

I wondered why this was the case. Are the dogs okay? Is there a repeat offender? Is it a dog? Is there something in the water? Are the people angry about something? Is this a form of protest? Lead by dogs or humans? I was in my thoughts for days, walking on more than just eggshells, watching every corner I turned. And then one day; I found the answer: so simple yet so key:

Trash cans. I was feeding my daily Nikotinabusus and noticed that there was no home for used cigarettes. No bins no nothing. There are trash cans, the ones with the wheels; that are often parked outside Apartments. But once you're out in the open, on offical street-territory, the bins disappear.

That, I assumed is the main cause for this dilemma, human laziness and incompitence of some set aside. And that is when i realized; Leipziger dogs and dog owners aren't as ruthless as we think: at the end of the day. Like us; who have no choice but to grow and age in Braunschweig, they are just victims of their circumstances, trying to make ends meet.

We visited multiple museums hoping to expand our art pallette. The one that struck me the most was the Museum of fine arts. It was a very modern and elegant place. I climbed the bulky stairs and explored all three floors. I sketched, walked and looked around. The majority of art that they had on show was antiquated art. Looking at my sketches, I'm definite that I was inspired and attracted to specific pieces with a similiar theme; human vulnerability and pain. I mainly sketched what I saw and free-styled the last two sketches from memory.
